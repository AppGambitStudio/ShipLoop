# SHIPLOOP — Company Type Analysis, Fixed vs Configurable and Onboarding

Spec Section 03

---

## 1. Four Company Types

ShipLoop has a fixed core and an adaptable context layer. Company type is the primary configuration axis. Agents don't change. The context they operate in does.

**Type A: Software Consulting/Service Company**
Sells expertise. Proof of work is the product.

**Type B: SaaS/Product Company**
Sells a product. Distribution is a pipeline.

**Type C: Solo Creator/Educator**
Sells attention and trust. Distribution IS the business.

**Type D: Indie Builder (Building in Public)**
Sells the journey. The process of building IS the content.

---

## 2. Software Consulting/Service Company

**Asset type:** Shipped tools, client outcomes, open-source projects, talks. The asset is demonstrated expertise.

**Brain dump example:** "Shipped CloudCorrect v2. Client used DocProof on 400 documents in one day. Accepted to speak at AWS re:Invent."

**Strategist goal:** Narrative coherence. Cloud + AI execution, not advice. Every post answers implicitly: why hire this team over a generic agency?

**Channel priority:** LinkedIn primary. GitHub for credibility. Reddit selectively. Low volume — one strong post per week.

**Signal:** Inbound enquiry rate. Profile visits from target companies. CTO/VP Engineering connection requests.

**Monologue character:** Narrative arcs and credibility gaps. Is the body of work telling one clear story?

**Silence threshold:** 2 weeks.

**Key insight:** A consulting/service company doesn't need volume. It needs coherence. Strategist ensures the last 10 posts collectively tell one story.

### What Distribution Looks Like Day-to-Day

A consulting/service company ships sporadically — maybe one significant thing per week (a tool release, a client milestone, a talk). The brain dumps are sparse but high-signal.

The engine's job is NOT to manufacture content on slow weeks. It's to ensure that when something IS shipped, it gets distributed with maximum narrative coherence. On quiet weeks, the Strategist may resurface older assets with new angles rather than pushing for fresh content.

**Typical week:**
- Monday: Brain dump about a client project milestone
- Tuesday-Thursday: No brain dump (deep client work)
- Friday: Brain dump about an open-source tool update
- Result: 1-2 posts, both reinforcing the same positioning thesis

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** Generating filler content ("5 tips for cloud architecture") on slow days. The Strategist's directives should explicitly deprioritise generic thought leadership for this company type.

### How the Voice Profile Differs

The consulting/service voice is:
- **Specific over general.** "We reduced this client's AWS spend by 40% using 3 specific checks" > "Here's how to optimise your cloud costs."
- **Proof over promise.** Show the output, not the capability. "We built X" > "We can build X."
- **Understated over promotional.** No exclamation marks. No "thrilled to announce." Just "Shipped. Here's what it does."

The Acting Agent's per-platform voice profile should reflect this — LinkedIn drafts for a consulting company sound fundamentally different from LinkedIn drafts for a SaaS company.

## 3. SaaS/Product Company

**Asset type:** Product features, user milestones, problem-solution insights, use case walkthroughs.

**Brain dump example:** "Shipped bulk export. Hit 500 active users. Churn call — user left because no Slack integration."

**Strategist goal:** Consistent top-of-funnel. Volume matters. SEO and community presence compound differently.

**Channel priority:** Twitter/X for daily presence. Reddit where ICP lives. Product Hunt for launches. Newsletter as owned channel.

**Signal:** Trial signups from content. Click-through to landing page. Keyword ranking movement.

**Monologue character:** Funnels and conversion events. Are we showing up where ICP searches?

**Silence threshold:** 1 week.

**Key insight:** Acting Agent must think in CTAs and awareness stages. Every draft has an implicit job: attract, educate, or convert.

### What Distribution Looks Like Day-to-Day

A SaaS founder ships constantly — feature updates, bug fixes, user milestones, churn insights. The brain dumps are frequent and varied.

The engine's job is to maintain consistent presence across multiple channels while ensuring content maps to the funnel: awareness → education → conversion.

**Typical week:**
- Monday: Brain dump about weekend feature shipped
- Tuesday: Brain dump about user milestone (500 active users)
- Wednesday: No brain dump
- Thursday: Brain dump about churn insight from support call
- Friday: Brain dump about competitor activity
- Result: 3-5 posts across Twitter, Reddit, and LinkedIn. Mix of feature announcements, user stories, and problem-solution threads.

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** All content being "we shipped X" announcements. The Strategist should ensure a mix: some posts attract (awareness), some educate (show how it solves the problem), some convert (CTA to try).

### How the Voice Profile Differs

The SaaS founder voice is:
- **Problem-first.** Open with the pain point, not the feature. "You know that thing where your CSV export takes 20 minutes? We fixed that." > "We added bulk export."
- **Building-in-public transparency.** Churn reasons, user feedback, roadmap decisions — shared openly. This builds trust and differentiates from faceless products.
- **Channel-appropriate CTAs.** Twitter ends with a link. Reddit provides value standalone (product mention is secondary). LinkedIn tells a user story.

### Content-to-Funnel Mapping

The Strategist should maintain awareness of where each draft sits in the funnel:

| Funnel Stage | Content Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Problem description, industry insight | "Most teams waste 3 hours/week on CSV exports" |
| Education | How-to, use case walkthrough | "Here's how [UserCo] processes 10K records in 2 minutes" |
| Conversion | Feature announcement with CTA | "Bulk export is live. Try it free." |

The weekly summary should show the mix: "This week: 2 awareness, 1 education, 0 conversion. Recommend adding a conversion post next week."

## 4. Solo Creator/Educator

**Asset type:** Insights, frameworks, lessons learned, student questions answered.

**Brain dump example:** "Realised today that most people learn new frameworks wrong. A student asked something I had no good answer for."

**Strategist goal:** Never go dark. Presence is the product. One missed week is noticed by the audience.

**Channel priority:** YouTube primary. Newsletter as owned channel. Twitter/X for daily presence. LinkedIn for cross-audience reach.

**Signal:** Subscriber growth rate. Email open rate and reply rate. Watch time. Comments showing depth.

**Monologue character:** Trust and consistency. How long since the last post? Is the creator's point of view coming through?

**Silence threshold:** 3 days.

**Key insight:** Strategist's most important function is sounding the alarm when silence has gone on too long.

### What Distribution Looks Like Day-to-Day

A solo creator's raw material is ideas and observations — they happen constantly but are ephemeral. A voice note captured while walking is often more valuable than a planned writing session.

The engine's job is to capture these moments and turn them into multi-format content. One strong insight should become a Twitter thread, a newsletter paragraph, and a YouTube short script — all from the same brain dump.

**Typical week:**
- Monday: Voice note brain dump (walking, stream of consciousness)
- Tuesday: Text brain dump (responding to a student question)
- Wednesday: No brain dump
- Thursday: Voice note (reaction to industry news)
- Friday: No brain dump
- Saturday: Text brain dump (weekend workshop insight)
- Result: 4-6 pieces across YouTube, Newsletter, Twitter, LinkedIn. Mix of contrarian takes, how-tos, and responses.

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** Over-polishing. The creator's audience follows for authenticity. A slightly rough voice note used as a YouTube short hook is more engaging than a scripted version of the same idea.

### How the Voice Profile Differs

The solo creator voice is:
- **Opinionated over balanced.** "Docs are repair manuals, not tutorials" > "There are different approaches to learning frameworks."
- **Conversational over polished.** Incomplete sentences are fine if that's how they talk. The engine should NOT clean up the voice.
- **Multi-format from single input.** One brain dump becomes 2-3 pieces. The Acting Agent should generate a Twitter thread AND a newsletter paragraph AND a YouTube short script from a single strong insight.

### The Burnout Risk

Solo creators are the most vulnerable to burnout because their silence threshold is shortest (3 days) and their audience notices absence fastest. The Burnout Protocol is most critical for this type:

- Evergreen bank should be populated during onboarding with at least 5-10 pre-approved pieces
- The nudge at Day 3 should feel supportive, not demanding ("Here are ideas from your recent comments — approve one if any feel right")
- Evergreen reposts should have a light contextual update ("Still getting questions about this 3 months later — the principle holds")

## 5. Indie Builder (Building in Public)

**Asset type:** The product itself (any stage — idea, MVP, launched), the process of building it, metrics, decisions, struggles, learnings. For indie builders, the journey IS the asset.

**Brain dump example:** "Spent 4 hours debugging Stripe webhooks — race condition causing duplicate charges. Fixed it with idempotency keys. Also hit 100 users yesterday. Thinking about raising prices from $9 to $19."

**Strategist goal:** Maintain a visible narrative of progress. The audience should be able to discover this builder today and understand the full journey. Momentum is the currency — silence breaks the story.

**Channel priority:** Twitter/X for daily presence and threads. Hacker News for technical depth and launches. IndieHackers for building-in-public updates and metrics. Dev.to/Hashnode for longer technical posts. Reddit selectively (r/SideProject, r/startups, niche subreddits).

**Signal:** Community engagement quality (substantive comments > drive-by upvotes). User signups attributed to content. DMs from potential users/collaborators. Follower growth in builder-audience segments.

**Monologue character:** Momentum and milestone narrative. Is the building-in-public story progressing? Are the posts getting more specific and valuable as the product matures?

**Silence threshold:** 10 days.

**Key insight:** For indie builders, the process IS the content. "I spent 4 hours debugging X" is more engaging than "shipped feature Y." The Strategist should prioritise authenticity and struggle-sharing alongside wins.

### What Distribution Looks Like Day-to-Day

Indie builders work in bursts — intense build sprints followed by quiet periods. Brain dumps are irregular and often raw.

The engine's job is to extract distribution material from the building process itself. Not just "I shipped X" but "I struggled with X, decided Y, learned Z."

**Typical week (active period):**
- Monday: Brain dump about weekend coding session and a bug fix
- Tuesday: No brain dump (heads-down building)
- Wednesday: Brain dump with a metrics update and a pricing decision
- Thursday-Friday: No brain dump (deep work)
- Saturday: Brain dump about a user feedback call
- Result: 2-4 posts across Twitter, HN, IndieHackers. Mix of debugging stories, milestone updates, and decision transparency.

**Typical week (sprint period):**
- No brain dumps all week. Engine in quiet mode.
- Engine doesn't panic — the 10-day silence threshold allows for build sprints.
- After sprint: one rich brain dump covers everything that happened. Noticing extracts 3-5 opportunities from the backlog.

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** Manufactured optimism. The audience follows for the real story. "Everything is going great!" when metrics are flat is worse than no post at all. The Strategist should encourage honesty, including sharing setbacks.

### How the Voice Profile Differs

The indie builder voice is:
- **Raw over polished.** "Spent 4 hours on this stupid bug" > "We resolved a challenging technical issue." The roughness is the voice.
- **Metric-included always.** Every substantive post should include a specific number: users, revenue, time spent, bug count. Numbers make building-in-public content credible.
- **Decision-transparent.** "I chose Postgres over MongoDB because..." > just shipping silently. The audience wants to understand the why.
- **Struggle-forward.** Leading with what's hard, not just what's working. "Here's what almost made me quit this week" > "Another great week of progress."

### Pre-Launch vs Post-Launch

The Indie Builder type supports both stages:

**Pre-launch (no users yet):**
- Content angles: building progress, technical decisions, design choices, "why I'm building this"
- Signal: community interest (comments, DMs asking "when is this launching?"), email list growth
- Strategist focuses on: building anticipation, finding the right community, testing messaging

**Post-launch (has users):**
- Content angles: user milestones, metrics updates, feature decisions based on feedback, debugging stories
- Signal: user signups from content, conversion from follower to user, community engagement depth
- Strategist focuses on: converting audience interest into users, maintaining momentum narrative

The Noticing Agent handles both — pre-launch brain dumps ("decided on the tech stack today") are extracted as process-as-content opportunities, while post-launch brain dumps ("hit 100 users") are extracted as milestone-update opportunities.

## 6. Fixed vs Configurable

### Fixed (same for all company types)
- Brain dump intake interface
- Noticing Agent extraction logic (same pipeline, different directives)
- Acting Agent drafting pipeline (same pipeline, different voice profiles)
- Approval UI and human touch points
- Posting infrastructure
- Core data schema
- Reviewing Agent signal collection loop

### Configurable (changes per company type)
- Strategist goal document (what winning looks like)
- Channel registry (platforms, communities, accounts)
- Signal definitions (what Reviewing Agent measures)
- Strategist monologue character (narrative arcs vs funnels vs cadence)
- Content angle defaults
- Posting cadence expectations
- Silence threshold and alarm conditions (14 / 10 / 7 / 3 days)
- Burnout Protocol settings (threshold, evergreen bank size)
- Confidence thresholds (service: 0.88/0.65, saas: 0.82/0.60, indie: 0.83/0.60, creator: 0.85/0.62)
- Process-as-content extraction priority (high for Indie Builder, low for others)
- Queue expiry window (default 48h, auto-adjusted from behaviour)

## 7. Onboarding as Configuration

Three questions at setup:

1. **Company type selection** — pre-fills all configurable parameters
2. **Goal statement** — what does winning look like in 90 days?
3. **Channel setup** — which platforms, communities, accounts connected

### Guided Goal Statement (for those who can't articulate one yet)

Not everyone can write a 90-day goal cold. For people who can't — especially indie builders and early-stage founders — the onboarding offers a guided path:

**Step 1: What are you trying to achieve?** (multiple choice)
- Get users for my product
- Get noticed in my industry
- Build an audience around what I'm building
- Attract clients for my services
- Something else (free text)

**Step 2: Who do you want to reach?** (multiple choice + free text)
- Technical founders / CTOs
- Indie devs / builders
- Non-technical decision makers
- General developer community
- A specific niche: ________

**Step 3: What does "good" look like?** (pre-filled templates per company type)
- *Service:* "Be known as the team that [does X], with [Y] inbound enquiries per month from [Z audience]"
- *SaaS:* "Get [X] trial signups per month from content, with [Y]% conversion to paid"
- *Creator:* "Grow to [X] subscribers, with [Y] engagement rate and [Z] revenue per subscriber"
- *Indie Builder:* "Build a following of [X] people who care about what I'm building, get [Y] users from my building-in-public content"

The guided version produces a goal statement that's good enough for the Strategist to start. The Strategist's quarterly review will refine it over time.

### Optional
- Competitor list (up to 3 competitors to monitor)
- Burnout Protocol threshold (days of silence before evergreen reposts activate)
- Evergreen bank (pre-approve a set of posts for silent mode)
- Launch Mode preparation (pre-draft response bank for launch events)

### What Onboarding Does NOT Ask

The onboarding should take < 10 minutes. These things are learned over time, not asked upfront:

- **Voice preferences** — learned from approvals, edits, and skips. Not a style questionnaire.
- **Posting schedule** — there is no schedule. The engine decides timing based on brain dumps and strategy.
- **Channel weights** — start equal, the Strategist adjusts based on signal data after the first 2-3 weeks.
- **Content angle preferences** — start with company type defaults, Strategist refines as signal accumulates.

## 8. Multi-User Path (v1 = Single User, Future = Team)

**v1 is single-user.** One person submits brain dumps, approves drafts, and reads summaries. This is the "distribution person" — even in a small team, one person owns the ShipLoop workflow.

**How small teams use v1:**
- The distribution person collects updates from teammates via Slack/standup/chat
- They consolidate into their brain dumps ("Alex shipped the API refactor, Sam closed the enterprise deal")
- The engine processes as if one person is doing everything
- Only the distribution person's voice is learned

**Future multi-user architecture (acknowledged, not built in v1):**
- Multiple brain dump sources (each team member submits independently)
- Voice profile per submitter OR a unified team voice profile (configurable)
- One shared approval queue with optional per-person routing
- Strategist operates at the team level, not individual level
- Role separation: submitters (brain dump only) vs approvers (approval queue access)

**What this requires in the schema (noted for future):**
- `user_id` FK on `input_dumps` (who submitted this)
- `user_id` FK on voice_profile_entries (whose voice is this learning)
- `user_config` becomes `team_config` with user membership
- Approval queue filtering by role

**v1 design principle:** Nothing in v1 should make multi-user impossible later. The schema should use `user_config.id` as a foreign key (not assume a single global config), and brain dumps should accept a `submitted_by` field even if v1 always fills it with the same user.

## 9. Cross-Type Strategist Comparison

| Dimension | Software Consulting/Service | SaaS/Product | Solo Creator/Educator | Indie Builder |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary currency** | Reputation | Pipeline | Trust | Momentum |
| **Strategist thinks in** | Narrative arcs | Funnels | Cadence | Milestones + journey |
| **Volume expectation** | Low (quality) | Medium (consistent) | High (never dark) | Burst (high around milestones, quiet during sprints) |
| **Worst failure** | Incoherent story | Inconsistent funnel | Going silent | Losing the narrative thread |
| **Key signal** | Right people noticing | Trial signups | Subscriber growth | Community engagement + user signups from content |
| **Content angle** | Proof of execution | Problem → solution | Insight → lesson | Process → progress (struggles + wins) |
| **Silence threshold** | 2 weeks | 1 week | 3 days | 10 days |
| **Primary channel** | LinkedIn | Twitter / Reddit | YouTube / Newsletter | Twitter / HN / IndieHackers |
| **Confidence threshold (auto)** | 0.88 | 0.82 | 0.85 | 0.83 |
| **Confidence threshold (review)** | 0.65 | 0.60 | 0.62 | 0.60 |
| **Voice character** | Understated, specific, proof-driven | Problem-first, transparent, CTA-aware | Opinionated, conversational, multi-format | Raw, metric-included, struggle-forward |
